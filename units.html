Category 1

Weight Units

Almost everything in steel trade is bought and sold by weight. These are the three primary weight units used across wholesale, retail, and mid-size orders in India.

MT

Metric Tonne

Used for large wholesale orders, inward stock from manufacturers (SAIL), and heavy items like beams and coils. 1 MT = 1,000 kg.

kg

Kilogram

Used for retail sales, smaller structural sections like angles and channels, or wire rods sold in small quantities.

Q

Quintal

1 Quintal = 100 kg. Common in Indian markets for mid-sized construction orders. Bridges the gap between kg and MT pricing.

Weight Unit Conversions
Unit Symbol Equal To Typical Use Case
1 Metric Tonne MT 1,000 kg / 10 Quintals Wholesale, SAIL inward stock, coils
1 Quintal Q / Qt 100 kg / 0.1 MT Mid-size construction orders
1 Kilogram kg 1,000 grams / 0.001 MT Retail, small orders, wire rods
💡 Tip for Purchase Managers: Always convert your requirement to MT before getting quotes — most wholesale price lists are published as ₹ per MT or ₹ per kg. Multiply kg rate by 1,000 to get the MT rate.
Category 2

Linear & Count Units

While price is weight-based, customers and site supervisors need to know "how many pieces" and "how many meters" they are receiving to reconcile with their structural drawings.

RM / RFT

Running Meters / Feet

Critical for TMT Re-bars (standard length 12 m) and MS Rounds. Engineers use total linear length from their blueprint.

Nos

Numbers (Pieces)

Essential for Angles, Channels, and Beams. A customer may order "20 pieces of 100×50 channel at 6 meters each."

Bdl

Bundles

TMT bars are transported in bundles. The number of rods per bundle varies by diameter (e.g., 8mm bundle has more rods than 16mm).

Category 3

Dimensional Units & Gauge

These units define the "size" of the product — ensuring customers receive exactly what the engineer has specified in structural drawings.

mm

Millimeters

Industry standard for thickness (plates, sheets) and diameter (TMT bars, MS Rounds). Engineers always specify in mm.

in / ft

Inches / Feet

Used for Roofing Sheet dimensions (length × width) and structural member lengths in local construction site communication.

G

Gauge (G)

Specifically for GP, GC, and Colour Roofing Sheets. Higher gauge = thinner sheet. E.g., 24G is thicker than 26G.

Category 4

Area Units

Used mainly for flat products like Roofing Sheets and MS Plates, where customers need to calculate how much material is required to cover a given area.

sq.ft

Square Feet

Most commonly used in local construction site conversations for roofing. "How many sq.ft of GC sheets do you need?"

sq.m

Square Meters

Used in architectural drawings and formal project specifications. 1 sq.m = 10.764 sq.ft.

Weight per Meter – TMT Re-bars

A trader must be able to convert a customer's requirement from meters of TMT bar to kilograms for billing. This table is the essential reference for that conversion.

TMT Re-bar: Weight per Running Meter (Formula: d²/162)
Diameter (mm) Weight per Meter (kg/m) Weight per 12m Bar (kg) Example: 100 meters = ?
8 mm 0.395 kg/m 4.74 kg 39.5 kg
10 mm 0.617 kg/m 7.40 kg 61.7 kg
12 mm 0.888 kg/m 10.66 kg 88.8 kg
16 mm 1.578 kg/m 18.94 kg 157.8 kg
20 mm 2.467 kg/m 29.60 kg 246.7 kg
25 mm 3.854 kg/m 46.25 kg 385.4 kg
32 mm 6.313 kg/m 75.76 kg 631.3 kg

* Formula: Weight (kg/m) = d² ÷ 162 (where d is diameter in mm). Values are approximate and may vary slightly by manufacturer.
