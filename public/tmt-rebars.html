TMT Re-bars Selection Guide

Choosing the right TMT bar for each structural element — diameter, grade, and application explained simply for builders, contractors, and engineers.

Home› Products› TMT Re-bars Guide
Step 1

Select by Diameter (Size in mm)

The diameter determines load-bearing capacity. Here is the standard application for residential and commercial projects:

Diameter (mm) Common Usage Why?
8mm – 10mm Stirrups (Rings) & Slabs Hold main bars together (rings) and for horizontal mesh in floor/roof slabs.
12mm – 16mm Beams & Columns The "backbone" of standard 1–3 story houses — primary vertical and horizontal support.
20mm – 25mm Foundations & Heavy Columns Underground footings and main pillars of high-rise buildings or bridges.
32mm+ Complex Infrastructure Reserved for dams, piers, canals, and massive industrial structures.
Step 2

Select by Grade (Strength & Ductility)

In India, you will mostly sell Fe 500, Fe 500D, or Fe 550. Here's what each grade means:

Fe 500 — Standard

The standard grade for most residential homes. Good balance of strength and affordability for everyday construction.

Fe 500D — High Ductility ⚡

The "D" stands for Ductility. These bars bend more without breaking, highly recommended for earthquake-prone zones — they absorb shock better.

Fe 550 / 600 — Industrial

Used for industrial projects with extremely high loads. Allows using slightly fewer bars for the same strength — ideal for factories, bridges, and high-rises.

Step 3

Usage by Structural Component

When a customer walks into OK Steels, advise them based on what they are building:

Rings / Stirrups

Prevent main vertical bars from buckling under pressure and keep them aligned during the concrete pour.

8mm10mm

Roof / Floor Slabs

Slabs need to be flexible enough to handle live loads (people, furniture) without cracking. Thin bars distribute load evenly.

8mm10mm12mm

Main Pillars (Columns)

Carry the entire weight of the building to the earth. Multi-story homes require heavier bars for adequate load distribution.

Min 12mm16mm20mm

Foundations & Footings

The underground base carrying the entire structure. High loads demand thicker, high-grade bars to resist soil pressure.

20mm25mm32mm

Quality Check

Quality Indicators for Your Clients

Remind your customers to look for these quality markers when purchasing TMT bars:

  • ✔
    Ribbing Pattern: Sharp, uniform ribs ensure better "grip" between the steel and concrete — poor ribs mean weaker bonding.
  • ✔
    ISI Mark & Grade Stamp: Ensure bars have the ISI mark and grade (e.g., SAIL Fe 500D) embossed directly on the bar. Verify against the test certificate.
  • ✔
    Bendability Test: High-quality TMT bars should bend 180° without showing any cracks on the outer surface.
  • ✔
    Weight per Meter: Cross-check actual weight per meter against the standard — under-weight bars indicate lower steel content and reduced strength.
💡

Pro-Tip for OK Steels Counter Sales

Many customers buy 8mm and 10mm in the highest volume for residential slabs and rings, but the 12mm and 16mm bars are your high-value items for the structural frame. Lead with the thicker bars when discussing total project cost.
